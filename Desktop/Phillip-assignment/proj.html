What is persistence?

Believe it or not, success really comes down to only one thing…and that one thing makes the biggest difference between people who succeed and people who don’t. It simply means finishing what you start and not giving up in the face of challenges.Persistence refers to perseverance in spite of fatigue or frustration.

So what is that one thing?

It’s having the grit and the persistence to just keep going, no matter what. Even when things get hard. Even when you make mistakes. Even when life feels impossible.

Persistence by dictionary definition is a firm or obstinate continuance in a course of action in spite of difficulty or opposition. Meaning throughout all of the trials and tribulations being thrown in to your path of success keeping a determined mind set you would be able to get through

How to stay Persistent

With obstacles can come the feeling of defeat It can be easy to fall to the face of defeat when your are tired and aren’t seeing the result that you were hoping to see. Ultimately feeling hopeless.There are are many ways to avoid this sabotaging mindset

  • Take A Break - When feeling tired take a break a short break away from the task at hand is not only good for you mind but if a little exercise is thrown into the mix like (walking,jogging,stationary exercises)it can also be very beneficial to your physical health
  • Try Not To Overwhelm- Over estimating how many task you can take on can cause you a lot of stress by limiting the amount of tasks you take on at once you would be able to complete your tasks Efficiently and without overworking yourself
  • Manage Your Time-By setting time aside for work and for Leisure you can give you time to work efficiently without burning yourself out and give you time to relax without taking to much relaxing time

The power of persistence

Before Kentucky Fried Chicken (KFC) was one of the world’s largest restaurants, Colonel Sanders drove all across the country from restaurant to restaurant cooking batches of chicken for restaurant owners and their employees looking for someone willing to purchase his chicken recipe. Colonel Sanders persisted through 1,009 rejections until he got a ‘yes’(Anthony Robbins, Unlimited Power, (New York: Simon & Schuster, 1986) After his first ‘yes,’ his franchising idea began to take off. By 1964, at age 74, Colonel Sanders had more than 600 franchised outlets for his chicken in the United States and Canada, and he sold his interest in the U.S. Company for $2 million ($13 million in 2006 dollars). KFC now does billions in sales each year and serves millions of customers daily in over 13,000 restaurants in 80 countries.

W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community that develops open standards to ensure the long-term growth of the Web.

The World Wide Web Consortium is the main international standards organization for the World Wide Web. Founded in 1994 and currently led by Tim Berners-Lee, the consortium is made up of member organizations that maintain full-time staff working together in the development of standards for the World Wide Web.
